# Contributing to AUMI

Thank you for your interest in contributing to the AUMI Human-Centered AI
Responsibility Framework.

AUMI is a curated governance and operations framework. Contributions are reviewed
to preserve conceptual integrity, practical relevance, and governance alignment.

This repository is not intended as a general-purpose template library.

---

## Purpose of This Repository

AUMI reflects real-world experience in designing, operating, and governing
AI systems in production environments.

It is published as a reference framework for:

- Responsible AI operations
- Risk-based governance
- Incident management
- Human oversight systems

All contributions should strengthen these goals.

---

## What Contributions Are Welcome

We welcome contributions that:

- Improve clarity, structure, or readability
- Correct factual or technical errors
- Strengthen governance alignment
- Add validated operational practices
- Improve consistency across documents

All submissions should be supported by practical experience,
industry standards, or credible references.

---

## What Contributions Are Not Accepted

We do not accept contributions that:

- Promote specific vendors, tools, or services
- Introduce unvalidated metrics or benchmarks
- Add marketing or promotional content
- Weaken governance or safety controls
- Duplicate existing material without improvement

Submissions that do not meet these criteria may be declined.

---

## Contribution Process

1. Fork the repository
2. Create a feature branch from `main`
3. Make focused, well-documented changes
4. Ensure consistency with existing structure and tone
5. Submit a pull request with clear justification

All pull requests are reviewed by maintainers.

---

## Review Criteria

Submissions are evaluated based on:

- Governance alignment
- Practical applicability
- Clarity and precision
- Consistency with AUMI principles
- Long-term maintainability

Maintainers may request revisions before acceptance.

All contributors are expected to follow the

## Questions and Discussion

For questions, open a GitHub Issue with the appropriate label.

Please do not use pull requests for general discussions.
